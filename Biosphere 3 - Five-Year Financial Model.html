Key Model Assumptions

1. Funding & Capitalization

The model assumes a phased approach to fundraising, aligning capital injection with key technical and commercial milestones. This multi-tranche strategy validates progress for investors and provides runway for major CapEx.

  • FY 2026 (Y1): $500k Seed (U. Arizona). Per prospectus, to establish the core engineering team (as contractors) and develop the initial platform architecture.
  • FY 2027 (Y2): $5M Series A (VCs, Strategics). To fund the LLC/SPV formation, hire the core team as FTEs, and finance the initial CapEx build-out of the autonomous lab MVP.
  • FY 2028 (Y3): $20M Series B (VCs, Strategics). To scale the physical platform, expand the engineering and BD teams, and provide runway to reach operational cash-flow positivity.

2. Revenue Model

Revenue generation begins in Y2 after the platform MVP is operational, diversifying from high-value subscriptions to strategic R&D contracts and long-term IP licensing.

  • Research Subscriptions: The core revenue driver. Assumes a growing base of corporate, national lab, and NGO clients paying an average of $250k-$600k/year for bespoke, autonomous research projects.
  • Strategic Partnerships: Assumes a major R&D contract (e.g., Project Prometheus, NASA) lands in Y3, providing non-dilutive R&D funding and platform validation.
  • IP Licensing: A conservative estimate of $500k in Y5 as discoveries made on the platform begin to be commercialized, with revenue shared with the University of Arizona.

3. Corporate & Personnel Structure

This structure is designed to insulate the University from liability while maximizing commercial agility and retaining a strategic connection for IP and facility access.

  • Y1 (Contractors): All 4 core team members are contractors, per user guidance, minimizing initial overhead and liability.
  • Y2 (LLC/SPV Formation): A separate legal entity is formed, funded by the Series A. Core team and new hires (8 FTEs total) become employees of this entity.
  • Personnel Scaling: Headcount grows to 40 FTEs by Y5, focusing on AI/Robotics engineers and a high-touch Business Development team.
  • B2 Access Fees: G&A costs include a significant, escalating fee paid from the B3 LLC *back to Biosphere 2 / U. Arizona* for facility access, acting as a direct financial return to the university.

Milestone & Funding Roadmap

This timeline connects the financial model (funding and spend) to the strategic milestones outlined in the Biosphere 3 prospectus. It demonstrates how capital is deployed to de-risk the venture and unlock value at each stage.

FY 2026 (Year 1): Foundational Team & Architecture

Funding: $500k (Seed)

Core engineering team (4 contractors) is assembled. Platform architecture, AI agent framework, and digital twin strategy are developed. Y1 burn is $475k, validating the need for the $500k seed round and creating urgency for the Series A.

FY 2027 (Year 2): MVP Platform & First Revenue

Funding: $5.0M (Series A)

LLC/SPV is formed. Core team hired as FTEs. $2M in CapEx is deployed to build the MVP autonomous lab. First two research "customers" (grants or small corporate) are signed, generating $500k in revenue. Strategic discussions with Project Prometheus begin.

FY 2028 (Year 3): Scale & Strategic Validation

Funding: $20.0M (Series B)

Series B provides capital to scale. Team doubles to 15 FTEs. Customer base expands to 5, and a major strategic partnership ($1M) is signed. The platform is now a robust, multi-tenant system. This is the key inflection point from R&D to a commercial enterprise.

FY 2029 (Year 4): Market Penetration

Funding: From Operations & Balance Sheet

$5M in CapEx is deployed for major platform expansion. Revenue grows 150% to $7.5M as the "research subscription" model is validated. The company has a strong cash balance of $12.5M, providing strategic flexibility.

FY 2030 (Year 5): Operational Profitability

Funding: Self-Sustaining

The business achieves operational profitability (EBITDA positive) with $3.5M in EBITDA on $17.5M in revenue. IP licensing revenue begins. With $13M in ending cash, the company is in a dominant position to lead the autonomous lab market, fund further growth, or consider an exit.
